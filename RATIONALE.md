# Rationale: Free to Personal upgrade landing

**Deliverable:** Interactive landing page at `/` (English), built as a standalone destination for paid/CRM traffic targeting long-term Free users.  
**Asset:** Full-page JPG in `public/submission/`.

---

## 1. Which Free vs paid difference did you lead with, and what makes it the first thing a viewer notices?

**Lead: unlimited synced devices**, expressed as freedom, not as a spec.

The hero pairs the headline *“Your second brain, without limits”* with a visual of **the same note open on a laptop, tablet, and phone**, marked “Synced”. Device sync is the difference a long-term Free user collides with most often (Free allows a single device, but these users move between desk, commute, and meetings all day), and it’s instantly understandable in one image, with no reading required. Three design choices make it the first thing noticed:

1. It’s the only illustration above the fold, positioned next to the headline.
2. The headline names the theme (“without limits”) rather than a feature name.
3. The subheadline immediately grounds it in the other concrete gains (offline, AI-powered search, 100 GB) with the transparent price.

Because the page serves **ad traffic, we don’t know an individual’s usage**, so the page never claims “you’ve hit your limit”. It sells the removal of the Free plan’s objective constraints instead.

**AI gets its own dedicated section.** AI-powered search is the most modern, differentiating item in the plan table, so it’s treated with the brand’s highest-contrast pattern: a dark panel with lime CTA (“Unlock AI search”), a working search mockup with highlighted matches, and three plain-language proof points. It is honest to the brief: the section is strictly about AI-powered search, the AI capability Personal actually unlocks.

Feature-to-benefit translation used across the page:

| Spec (plan table) | How the page says it |
|---|---|
| Unlimited devices | Your second brain, on every device |
| Offline access | Work from anywhere, even with no signal |
| AI-powered search | Search that finds what you mean |
| 100 GB / 200 MB | Save without stopping |

The full plan table (Free vs Personal) appears later for the rational check, with Personal marked “Recommended”.

---

## 2. What drove your creative decisions (landing page vs video)?

I chose **Option B: landing page** because:

1. **The decision needs a surface, not a mood.** Upgrading is a considered choice: the page walks a narrative arc from recognition (hero) to what changes (3 benefit cards), AI showcase (dark section), before/after, an honest comparison table, FAQ, and close. A silent video can build desire but can’t host the comparison and objection-handling this audience needs.

2. **No pressure, by design.** The brief asks for a decision that feels easy and natural: there’s no countdown, no fake scarcity; “Stay on Free” and “Compare plans” are always visible; the FAQ answers the real fears (“Will I lose my notes?”, “Can I go back?”). Nothing outside the plan table is promised.

3. **Brand-native craft.** The page reuses the design system reverse-engineered from evernote.com (cream canvas, near-black CTAs hovering to brand green, dark AI panel with lime accent, soft shadows, calm 300 ms motion). It reads as Evernote, not as a generic promo template.

4. **Micro-copy that lowers friction:** CTA verbs are “Continue without limits” / “Unlock everything” instead of “Upgrade”; price framed as **€7.99/month, less than two coffees**; trust line “Trusted by 250 million users” sourced from the product description in the brief.

---

## 3. How would you know if this asset worked?

**Primary signal: click-through rate on the primary CTA (“Continue without limits” / “Unlock everything”) into checkout start**, measured on Free users landing from the campaign.

Why this one:

- It measures **intent to pay**, not curiosity: the exact behavior the asset exists to create.
- It’s attributable per traffic source and segmentable by tenure (e.g. 2+ years on Free), so we can verify the message resonates with the target segment specifically.
- It isolates the page’s persuasion job from checkout friction (which belongs to the funnel step after).

**Supporting diagnostics (optimization, not success):** scroll depth reaching the comparison table, opens of the “Will I lose my notes?” FAQ (fear signal), and CTR split between hero CTA and table CTA to learn whether emotion or comparison converts more.
